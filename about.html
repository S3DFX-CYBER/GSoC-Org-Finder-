Your Smart Guide to GSoC 2026

FindMyGSoC helps students — especially first-timers — discover the right Google Summer of Code organization, explore beginner-friendly issues, and land their first open-source contribution.

apps

What is this app?

FindMyGSoC is a free, open-source web dashboard that aggregates live data from the Google Summer of Code 2026 program. It lets you search, filter, and compare participating organizations by tech stack, category, difficulty, and competition level — all in one place.

Built for students who are new to GSoC and don't know where to start, the app removes the guesswork and helps you find projects that match your skills and interests in minutes.

Quick-Start Guide

New to GSoC? Follow these 4 steps to get started.

1

Check the Timeline

Start by reviewing the GSoC 2026 timeline so you know when applications open, close, and when results are announced.

2

Use AI Recommender or Search

Enter your skills in the AI Recommender or use the search filters to find organizations that match your tech stack and interests.

3

Bookmark & Compare

Add shortlisted organizations to your Watchlist, then explore their good-first issues and mentors to evaluate fit.

4

Start Contributing

Pick a good-first issue, make your first contribution, and reach out to mentors before the application window opens.
